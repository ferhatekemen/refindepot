DIFFERENCES BETWEEN "REFERENCE LETTER" AND "LETTER OF RECOMMENDATION"+_

McGill, Canada's leading university, states the differences between "Reference Letter" and "Letter of Recommendation" as follows: *

  • the authority addressed by the reference,
  • contextual details, and
  • its special purpose


the created document is called either "Reference Letter" or "Letter of Recommendation".

The "Reference Letter" is more general and does not address a specific person or organization. Usually these letters begin as "Attention to the Person concerned" or "Dear Sir / Madam" and are handed directly to the student and stored for future use by the student.


Where to be used:

  1. Character Assessment
  2. For academic purposes
  3. Employment-Related
  4. General Purpose


“Letter of Recommendation” ; It is specially requested for a certain employment position, academic program, scholarship or award application. Usually, these letters are not sent to the applicant requesting it, but to the institution where the letter will be used and will not be seen by the student or the person requesting the letter.


These can be categorized as follows:

  1. Employment-Related examples could be a letter written for a job application, faculty position, research assistant, etc.
  2. For Academic Admission: Some of the universities in the world request a Letter of Recommendation in their academic acceptance.
  3. For official approval or validation purposes: For example, a letter that confirms and verifies the scholarship of a student who has been approved for it or to nominate a student for a scholarship (Nomination Letter)

*(Last updated: April 13, 2020)

As it can be seen in the explanations made so far, a letter with a general academic evaluation and not written to a specific institution and/or person is a reference letter. On the other hand, a letter written for a specific academic position, research assistant, etc. and addressed to an individual or an institution, including details about the position applied for is called the "Letter of Recommendation".
